# Commercial Model

Med-AgentLab can evolve from an academic prototype into a hybrid intelligence product for qualitative clinical research. The commercial direction should preserve the system's human-in-the-loop philosophy: AI accelerates analysis, while humans remain responsible for interpretation, ethics and final decisions.

## Target Users

- University research groups.
- Clinical research teams.
- Graduate students working with qualitative interview data.
- Hospitals or research centers that need local/private analysis workflows.
- Advisors who review student or institutional qualitative research outputs.

## Value Proposition

- Faster first-pass qualitative coding.
- Downloadable academic reports.
- Local privacy preprocessing through Ollama.
- API fallback pools to reduce quota and outage risk.
- PubMed-assisted validation for clinical theme checking.
- Transparent logs, router events and human review points.

## Possible Editions

### Community Edition

- Open-source local prototype.
- Single-user workflow.
- Manual setup with `.env`.
- Suitable for academic demonstration and controlled experiments.

### Academic Pro Edition

- Easier installation.
- Better report templates.
- Project history and export management.
- Researcher/supervisor review workflow.

### Institutional Edition

- Local or private-cloud deployment.
- Stronger audit logs.
- Configurable privacy rules.
- Admin-controlled model/provider policies.
- Support and deployment consulting.

## Revenue Options

- Installation and deployment consulting.
- Academic lab support packages.
- Institution-specific customization.
- Private deployment and maintenance.
- Training workshops for hybrid human + AI qualitative analysis.

## Hybrid Intelligence Positioning

The product should not be marketed as a fully autonomous clinical decision tool. A stronger commercial position is:

> Med-AgentLab helps researchers produce structured qualitative analysis drafts while keeping human experts in control of interpretation, approval and publication.

## Risks

- Handling real patient data requires strict governance.
- External model APIs may create privacy and compliance limitations.
- Generated themes can be incomplete or wrong.
- Commercial claims must avoid clinical decision support promises unless the product is redesigned and validated for that regulatory category.
